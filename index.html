Key Issues

Cost of Living

Prices, food security, and household pressure

Rising prices, food affordability, electricity costs, and daily essentials remain among the most visible pressures on Filipino households.

Corruption and Accountability

Governance, transparency, and public trust

Concerns about graft, misuse of public funds, and weak enforcement continue to shape how people view institutions and leadership.

Jobs and Wages

Work quality, pay, and economic mobility

Beyond employment numbers, this issue area focuses on underemployment, insecure work, wage growth, and the challenge of earning enough to keep up with daily expenses.

Poverty and Hunger

Social protection and unequal access

Poverty reduction, hunger, and access to support programs remain central to the country's long-term development and social stability.

Climate and Disasters

Extreme weather, resilience, and adaptation

Floods, typhoons, heat, drought, and disaster preparedness are recurring national concerns with strong local consequences.

Disinformation and Digital Life

Truth, platforms, and online influence

Social media misinformation, online manipulation, and platform accountability are now major public-interest issues that shape elections, policy debates, and trust.

Philippine Issues

Transport and Infrastructure

Mobility, roads, rail, and public service delivery

Commuting costs, traffic, public transport quality, flood control, and infrastructure execution all shape how people experience the state in everyday life.

Education

Learning gaps, school quality, and student futures

Schools remain a major national concern through classroom shortages, learning recovery, affordability, and the long-term impact on opportunity.

Health

Hospitals, vaccines, mental health, and access to care

Public health capacity, medicine costs, local health services, and preventive care remain critical to community well-being.

Agriculture and Food

Farm livelihoods, supply chains, and food stability

Farmers, fisheries, rice supply, and climate-sensitive production directly affect inflation, rural livelihoods, and national food security.

Public Safety and Justice

Crime, rule of law, and institutional response

Safety, policing, case resolution, and the consistent application of the law continue to shape public confidence and daily security.

Housing and Urban Life

Shelter, utilities, and livable communities

Rent, relocation, informal settlements, water access, and urban planning affect both dignity and long-term quality of life.

Territory and National Security

West Philippine Sea and national sovereignty

Territorial integrity, maritime tensions, and defense readiness remain visible national issues with diplomatic and economic consequences.

OFWs and Migration

Overseas workers, remittances, and protection abroad

Migrant labor continues to support the economy while raising questions about labor protection, family separation, and government support systems.

Youth and Civic Participation

Representation, voice, and democratic engagement

Young Filipinos are increasingly central to debates on voting, organizing, digital discourse, and the future direction of public life.
